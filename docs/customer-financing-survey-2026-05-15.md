# Mammoth Tree Service customer financing survey

_Date: 2026-05-15_

## Use case

Mammoth Tree Service needs customer payment-plan options for larger or unexpected tree-work jobs, especially:

- Tree removal
- Hazardous/emergency removals
- Storm cleanup
- Tree trimming/pruning
- Stump grinding

Best-fit financing should support a small local service contractor, be easy to explain on the website, and avoid making Mammoth responsible for underwriting, collections, or in-house payment plans.

## Recommended shortlist

### 1) Wisetack — best first call / strongest tree-service fit

**Why it fits Mammoth**

- Directly positioned for home service businesses.
- Multiple live tree-service companies advertise Wisetack specifically for tree work.
- Official Wisetack home-services page says financing is available from **$500–$25,000** with terms from **3–120 months**.
- Customers can check options in under a minute, and eligibility check does not impact credit.
- Merchant pays a flat per-transaction fee, with no setup/subscription cost listed on the official home-services page.
- Contractor gets paid after the work is completed; Wisetack says funds land in **1–3 business days**.

**Important limitations / questions**

- $25,000 limit should cover most local tree work, but huge land-clearing/storm jobs may exceed it.
- Need to confirm merchant transaction fee for Mammoth specifically.
- Need to confirm whether stump grinding, trimming, land clearing, and storm cleanup are all approved categories under Mammoth’s merchant account.

**Evidence**

- Official Wisetack home-services page: https://www.wisetack.com/merchants/home-services
- Official consumer FAQ: https://www.wisetack.com/faqs
- Tree-service examples using Wisetack:
  - GM 2 Tree Services: https://gm2treelandscapingllc.com/financing/
  - Tree Work Now: https://treeworknow.com/flexible-financing.php
  - Scott’s Treewurk: https://scottstreewurk.com/financing/
  - Branching Out Tree Service: https://intreeservice.com/financing

**Recommendation**

Start here. Wisetack has the cleanest match to the website message: “Financing available for qualifying tree-service projects.”

---

### 2) Acorn Finance — best no-dealer-fee backup / marketplace option

**Why it fits Mammoth**

- Contractor page says $0 dealer fee financing, no subscription/sign-up/credit card fees, same-day setup, and loans up to **$100,000** with terms up to **20 years**.
- Customers compare multiple lender offers through one application/link.
- Prequalification can happen without impacting credit.
- Contractor gets a shareable custom financing link that can be used on estimates, invoices, website, email, text, or QR code.

**Why it may be less ideal than Wisetack**

- It is more of a loan marketplace than an embedded home-services payment option.
- Customer experience may feel less tightly tied to the contractor invoice.
- Some customers may receive offers with higher APRs depending on credit.

**Evidence**

- Official contractor page: https://www.acornfinance.com/contractors/

**Recommendation**

Good backup if Wisetack fees or limits are not attractive, or if Mammoth wants higher-limit financing for very large jobs.

---

### 3) Hearth — useful if Mammoth also wants estimating/contracts/invoicing software

**Why it fits Mammoth**

- Built for home improvement pros.
- Offers financing, digital quotes, contracts, invoices, and payments.
- Public app-store/search snippets list loans from **$1,000–$100,000**, terms from **2–12 years**, multiple lenders, no dealer fees, and preapproval without credit-score impact.

**Why I would not choose it first**

- It appears to be a broader subscription/software platform, not just a simple financing plug-in.
- More tool than Mammoth needs if the immediate goal is simply adding “monthly payment options” to the site.
- Online contractor commentary is mixed enough that I’d vet hard before relying on it.

**Evidence**

- Official home page: https://www.gethearth.com/
- Pricing page: https://www.gethearth.com/pricing

**Recommendation**

Consider only if Mammoth wants to replace/improve its estimate/contract/payment workflow at the same time.

---

### 4) GreenSky — strong for large home-improvement contractors, probably heavy for Mammoth at first

**Why it fits**

- Well-known home-improvement financing brand.
- Official page says credit limits up to **$100,000** and fixed-rate/promotional plan options.
- Designed for point-of-sale contractor financing.

**Why I would not choose it first**

- More enterprise/home-improvement dealer style.
- Dealer fees and compliance complexity can be more of a concern.
- Better fit for HVAC, roofing, windows, remodeling, pools, etc. than a small local tree-service company unless Mammoth is doing consistently large-ticket work.

**Evidence**

- Official home-improvement page: https://www.greensky.com/home-improvement/

**Recommendation**

Keep as a later option if Mammoth grows into frequent high-ticket jobs and wants broader promotional financing.

---

### 5) Regions Home Improvement Financing / former EnerBank — credible, but probably second-stage

**Why it fits**

- Regions is Alabama-based and credible.
- Official page says contractors can offer mobile, online, or phone loan applications; credit decisions can happen in minutes; funding can usually be deposited within 24 hours after authorization.
- Page says there are no fixed or required contractor costs, though many popular loan products have dealer fees.

**Why I would not choose it first**

- More traditional contractor-financing program.
- Dealer-fee model may complicate pricing and margins.
- Better for larger established home-improvement contractors than quick first website rollout.

**Evidence**

- Official contractor financing page: https://www.regions.com/small-business/contractor-financing

**Recommendation**

Worth calling because of Alabama/Regions familiarity, but I would not build the first Mammoth website financing message around it unless terms are clearly better than Wisetack/Acorn.

---

### 6) Enhancify — promising on fees/limits, but needs vetting

**Why it fits**

- Home-improvement financing platform.
- Official page claims financing up to **$200,000**, instant preapproval/decisions, next-business-day payment, 0% promotional options, and “no dealer fees” messaging.
- Has landscaping/outdoor-related case studies, not just HVAC/roofing.

**Why to be cautious**

- Strong sales/commission claims need direct verification.
- Need to confirm exactly how customers receive funds, who pays whom, whether tree work is an accepted category, and whether there are subscription or program costs.

**Evidence**

- Official home page: https://www.enhancify.com/

**Recommendation**

Good to contact, but do not make it first choice until pricing and customer experience are verified.

---

### 7) Service Finance Company — credible but not likely first choice

**Why it fits**

- Nationally licensed sales finance company.
- Official site says they finance 500+ home improvement products/services including roofing, HVAC, remodeling, flooring, fences, awnings, sheds, and pools.

**Why not first**

- Official category list does not clearly mention tree services.
- Feels more like traditional dealer financing for established home-improvement verticals.

**Evidence**

- Official services page: https://www.svcfin.com/services

**Recommendation**

Only pursue if Wisetack/Acorn/Regions/Enhancify are unsuitable.

## Website messaging recommendation

Use cautious, conversion-oriented wording until a provider is selected and approved.

### Homepage / services CTA

> Need tree work now but prefer not to pay all at once? Ask about monthly payment options for qualifying tree removal, trimming, storm cleanup, and stump grinding projects.

### Short financing section

> Financing may be available for qualifying tree-service projects. If you’re dealing with a dangerous tree, storm damage, or a larger removal job, we can discuss payment options during your free estimate.
>
> Financing is subject to credit approval. Terms vary by provider and applicant.

### Estimate form checkbox

> I’m interested in financing / monthly payment options if available.

### If Wisetack is selected

> Fast, simple financing may be available for qualifying purchases from $500 to $25,000 through Wisetack. Checking your options does not impact your credit score. Financing is subject to credit approval; terms may vary.

Confirm the exact required Wisetack disclosure before publishing final provider-specific language.

## My recommendation

For Mammoth’s first website financing rollout:

1. **Call/apply with Wisetack first.** It is the cleanest match for tree service and already appears on other tree-service sites.
2. **Also apply/check Acorn Finance.** It gives Mammoth a no-dealer-fee backup and higher loan ceiling.
3. **Call Regions Home Improvement Financing as a local/credible comparison.** Useful because Regions is familiar in Alabama, but watch dealer fees.
4. **Do not start with GreenSky/Service Finance unless Mammoth is doing frequent large-ticket jobs.** They may be overkill.
5. **Ask Enhancify for terms but verify carefully.** The claims are attractive, but I would not anchor the site around them without a direct terms sheet.

## Due diligence questions before choosing

Ask each provider:

1. Are tree removal, stump grinding, tree trimming, and storm cleanup eligible services?
2. What are the loan limits and minimum ticket size?
3. Does the customer prequalify with a soft credit pull?
4. When does a hard pull occur?
5. What fees does Mammoth pay: dealer fee, transaction fee, subscription, setup, monthly fee?
6. When does Mammoth get paid?
7. Can Mammoth take deposits before financing funds?
8. Is funding tied to job completion/customer confirmation?
9. Do they provide approved website copy/disclosures?
10. Are there Alabama-specific requirements?
11. Can the financing link be placed on the website and estimate forms?
12. What happens if a customer cancels or disputes after approval?

## Bottom line

Financing is a good conversion lever for Mammoth, especially for hazardous removals and storm cleanup where the customer may not have budgeted for the job. The safest rollout is to add generic “monthly payment options may be available” language now, then switch to provider-specific Wisetack/Acorn wording once Mammoth is approved and has the required disclosure language.
